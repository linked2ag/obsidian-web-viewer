Obsidian Beginner Guide

1. What is a "Vault" & How to Open It

  • What is a Vault? Think of a "Vault" simply as a standard folder on your computer's drive. Inside are your text notes (as simple .md Markdown files) and folders. Obsidian leaves them as regular files on your drive, keeping your data secure and fully yours.
  • Opening your Vault at start: When launching the app for the first time, click the purple "Select Vault Folder" button. Select and confirm your prepared notes folder in your computer's file window, and the tool will automatically load all your notes.
  • Switching Vaults during work: To open a different folder while working, click the icon with the upward-pointing arrow ("Load Different Vault Folder") at the bottom of the far-left gray sidebar to load any other folder.

2. Local Relations & Graph Map

  • Local Relations (Bottom Left): Shows links connected to your active note. Outgoing Connections shows which notes are linked from here. Backlinks lists other notes linking back to this one. Clicking any note name opens it immediately.
  • Graph View (Visual Map): A fully interactive map showing notes as dots (nodes) and links as lines. Open it via the network icon (three connected dots) in the left sidebar.
    • Pan & Zoom: Click & drag empty space to move. Scroll to zoom in/out.
    • Move Dots: Click & drag individual dots to rearrange the map.
    • Highlight Connections: Click once on a dot. It turns purple, its links light up, and its direct neighbors' names turn bold and black (while others fade) to visualize relationships.
    • Clear Highlight: Click any empty blank space on the map to reset all colors.
    • Filter Folders & Labels: Use the toolbar buttons at the top of the Graph View to filter which folders appear or to toggle text labels on/off.
    • Open Note: Click directly on the written name of a note to open it.

3. Tabs – Working Like a Web Browser

  • Switching Tabs: Click on any tab name at the top to display that note or the Graph View, just like in Google Chrome or Safari.
  • Closing Tabs: Right-click any tab to open a menu to close that tab (Close Tab) or clean up the rest of your workspace (Close Others).
  • Open in New Tab: Clicking a file in the sidebar opens it in the active tab. To open it in a new tab, hold down Ctrl (Windows) or Cmd (Mac) while clicking.

4. WikiLinks & Repairing Broken Links

  • Internal Links: Write links to other notes in double square brackets, e.g., [[My Shopping List]]. In reading mode, this becomes a clickable blue link.
  • What is a Broken Link? If you link to a note that doesn't exist yet (e.g., [[Future Plan 2027]]), the link appears red and dashed (pointing to nothing).
  • How to Repair a Broken Link:
    • Create Page: Click the red link and choose "Create Target Page" to instantly create a new, empty file with that name.
    • Redirect Link: Click the red link, choose an existing note (Step 1), select an optional heading/chapter (Step 2), and click to automatically redirect the link.
    • Central Control Panel: Click the broken chain icon in the far-left sidebar. If there are broken links, it glows red. Open it to scan and easily fix all broken links in your workspace from one convenient menu.
